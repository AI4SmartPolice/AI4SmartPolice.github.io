    AI for Smart Police focuses on AI-based smart policing applications, with in-depth innovations primarily in the anti-drug direction, encompassing anti-drug intelligence analysis, efficient toxic substance detection, and molecular toxicology analysis. We collaborate deeply with institutions such as the Key Laboratory of Drug Monitoring and Control, Drug Intelligence and Forensic Center, Ministry of Public Security (DIFC) and the Department of Forensic Medicine at Zhongshan School of Medicine, Sun Yat-sen University (SYSU-ZSSOM). Additionally, our research extends to related smart policing areas including anti-money laundering, anti-fraud, and deepfake detection.

Pengfei Liu concurrently serves as an intern at Peng Cheng Laboratory (PCL)â€”Intelligent Computing Department, Cloud Brain Research Institute, as a joint PhD student mentored by Associate Researcher Zhixiang Ren, head of the Intelligent Computing Application Research Office at Cloud Brain Institute (research direction: scientific applications based on large-scale intelligent computing systems), and is a member of the Peng Cheng Cloud-brain Project (PCL-brain).

    Interpretable AI for Anti-Drug and Molecular Analysis: Leveraging large-scale intelligent computing systems and deep learning technologies to enhance the model's representation capabilities for complex data, while employing visual analytics to boost the interpretability and scientific credibility of model outcomes. We realize AI-based tasks in molecular science and extend them to policing applications, such as rapid detection of new psychoactive substances (NPS) in collaboration with the DIFC, and toxicological analysis in partnership with the SYSU-ZSSOM.
